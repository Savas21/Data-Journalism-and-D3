Among Americans, Smoking Decreases As Income Increases.

The Relationship Between Income And Smoking In The United States

In this project, I'm analyzing the trends shaping people's lives back to 2014, as well as creating charts, graphs and interactives to help understand the findings. The data we're using collected from American Community Survey (ACS) and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS).

You can directly explore the convincing correlation by clicking on any of each axis titles.

Generally, among Americans, smoking decreases as income increases. (When you select 'Household Income (Median)' and 'Smokes (%)' at the same time.)

Nationwide, one of the interesting facts is when we select household income as a fixed value, the smokes (%) and alcohol consumption (%) are opposite. The household income at 50,000 has some tiny change, but the States at 40,000 and 70,000 are switching their positions.

Also, it is interesting to see the state of Puerto Rico is getting clustered out from the central states in America in the most of the indicators.
